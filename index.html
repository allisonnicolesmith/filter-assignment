Councilman Shuns Real Estate Money in Race for New York City Comptroller After Already Amassing Over $130k

Despite recent rhetoric to the contrary, Brad Lander has won support from leaders in big real estate and development.

By Allison Nicole Smith| March 9, 2020

Brad Lander, the city councilmember representing District 39 in Brooklyn and the leading candidate in the New York City race for city comptroller, is one of many progressive candidates who has pledged to refuse campaign contributions from “corporate PACs or lobbyists, or from for-profit real estate developers or landlords.” However, Lander is subsidizing his campaign with real estate donations he’s collected from past elections.

The pledge he’s made is largely superficial given that the comptroller doesn’t play a role in city’s ULURP process. But tracking his campaign finance records is nevertheless important because it shows how candidates adopt progressive talking points to appeal to leftist voters, and in this particular case, it connects to the critical role Lander played in pushing forward the the controversial Gowanus rezoning project in spite of community protest.

The city’s Campaign Finance Board’s website shows the term-limited legislator has raised just over $3.5 million, an amount that the city’s taxpayer-funded 8-to-1 matching program largely contributed to. Without public funds, Lander has received $816,593 in campaign contributions. The CFB website also shows that he’s rolled over $36,740 in funds from previous election campaigns. While Lander has not received any funds from real estate developers or landlords, he has accepted over $3,000 in contributions from individuals in the real estate industry.

In 2017, Lander raised over $400k, approximately $130,000 of which came from the real estate industry.

Lander’s largest total contributions came from Two Trees Management, totaling $7,750. While $2,000 came from company executives, the remaining $5,750 came from employee relatives.

Lander’s largest number of contributions came from The Related Companies, a privately owned real estate firm that’s best known for the controversial Hudson Yards project. Twenty-two executives at Related contributed a total $6,250, most of which were bundled by Jay Kriegel, who was a Senior Adviser at the firm.

Another notable private real estate developer that has contributed to Lander’s campaigns is Silverstein Properties. Company employees contributed $2,250, while $3,500 came from relatives of the firm’s employees, totaling $5,500 altogether.

Lander has received thousands in donations from other developer firms, though these were the largest. In addition to private developers, Lander has also received $11,350 from PR firms associated with furthering developer interests.
